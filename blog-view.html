Overview

What is QuickStay?

QuickStay is a PHP and MySQL hotel management system that brings reservations, room availability, billing, and user roles into a single workflow.

Booking operations

Customers can search rooms, create bookings, pay online, and view booking history from one flow.

Hotel management

Clerks and managers can handle room allocation, walk-ins, check-in, checkout, invoices, and reports.

Problem

Why the system exists

Hotels that rely on separate tools for bookings, billing, and staff access create friction at every handoff.

Operational gap

Manual records make simple hotel work feel heavy.

QuickStay exists because booking, billing, room status, guest details, and staff access need to move together instead of living in separate notes and spreadsheets.

Operational Impact

Designed workflow outcomes

These are architecture-level outcomes the system is designed to support, not made-up post-launch analytics.

Lower room-conflict risk

Centralizing room availability around the reservation record reduces the chance of front-desk and online booking channels assigning the same room twice.

Stronger billing accuracy

Keeping booking totals, services, taxes, discounts, advance payments, and final checkout charges inside one invoice flow reduces missed financial steps.

Cleaner staff separation

Role-specific workspaces let managers, clerks, customers, and travel companies act on the same data without sharing the same controls.

Engineering View

How data and edge cases move through the system

A hotel platform feels trustworthy only when reservation records, invoices, payments, and room status changes stay linked from start to finish.

Reservation to revenue flow

1

Reservation created

The booking record ties together guest or company data, room selection, stay dates, and status.

2

Invoice generated

Room rate, extra services, taxes, discounts, and advance payments attach to the stay instead of living in separate notes.

3

Payment recorded

Stripe checkout updates the payment state, while production-grade handling should trust verified webhook events as the final source of truth.

4

Checkout closes the stay

Final billing locks the stay record and reopens room availability for the next guest.

Operational edge-case thinking

1

Double-booking control

Availability should be checked before a reservation is written and checked again before room assignment at check-in.

2

Failed payment handling

Abandoned or failed payments should keep the booking in a pending or unpaid state instead of marking it confirmed from the return URL alone.

3

Role-limited corrections

Cancellation, late-arrival adjustments, and final checkout changes should stay inside authorized staff workflows, not customer-facing views.

Payments and extensions

payments, companies, and guest assignments

These supporting records extend the base reservation model for online payments, corporate stays, and multi-guest bookings.

payments.invoice_idlinks payment events back to invoice state
payments.stripe_session_idstores checkout session reference
payments.statuspending, paid, failed, refunded

guest_assignments.reservation_idmaps named guests into a company booking

Reservation links users and rooms

reservations.user_id connects the person or company making the booking, while reservations.room_id ties the stay to a specific room record and availability state.

Invoice links reservation and payments

invoices.reservation_id keeps room charges and services attached to the stay, and payments.invoice_id tracks how that invoice was actually paid.

Companies and services extend the base model

Corporate bookings and extra services should extend the core reservation instead of duplicating it, so reporting and billing still resolve back to one source of truth.

Security

Built with protection layers

Session-based auth

Session timeout, HttpOnly cookies, SameSite cookies, and session regeneration after login.

Password and SQL safety

Passwords use PHP hashing, and database writes use prepared statements and CSRF tokens.
